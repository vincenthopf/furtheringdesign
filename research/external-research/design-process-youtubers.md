## Findings

### Executive Summary

Several key YouTube channels and creators actively share their design processes through live streams and recorded videos, catering to a wide range of design disciplines. For web design and freelancing, Flux Academy (Ran Segall) offers live Q&As and challenges focusing on Webflow and business aspects. DesignCourse (Gary Simon) specializes in UI/UX, providing live critiques and redesigns of viewer submissions. A unique trend in competitive design is showcased by Relume with its 'Relume Design League' (RDL), an esports-style competition for web designers in Figma. In brand and logo design, Will Paterson and The Futur (Chris Do) offer deep critiques and process walkthroughs, with Paterson's channel reaching 1 million subscribers. Other notable creators include Pierluigi Giglio, who focuses on Framer interactions; Sketch Together, for daily UI and illustration practice; and CharliMarieTV, who breaks down her process for web and brand design projects. Even company channels like Framer contribute by demonstrating the reasoning behind their tool's interaction architecture.

### Value Of Observing Design Processes

Observing these creators offers significant educational benefits by exposing the raw, unedited thinking behind design decisions. For instance, Gary Simon on DesignCourse narrates his real-time fixes for layout, typography, and spacing on viewer-submitted designs, providing a masterclass in visual hierarchy. Ran Segall at Flux Academy discusses client context, pricing, and content strategy while building websites, connecting design choices to business goals. This direct observation of problem-solving, tool mastery, and strategic trade-offs is invaluable for designers looking to move beyond tutorials and understand the 'why' behind the work.

### Design Challenge And Esports Trend

A growing trend in the design community is the gamification of design skills through live, competitive challenges, effectively creating an 'esports' format for designers. The most prominent example is the 'Relume Design League' (RDL), hosted on the Relume YouTube channel. In this format, two designers compete head-to-head in Figma, given a specific theme (e.g., 'Feminine Organic Skincare') and a strict time limit to create a design. These live-streamed events, such as the 'RDL 2 - FINAL: Joseph Berry vs Dan Mall,' showcase the entire creative process under pressure, from rapid ideation and wireframing to final high-fidelity execution. This format is a goldmine for observing how experienced designers make quick decisions about hierarchy, layout, and component structure when faced with tight constraints, offering a unique and entertaining educational experience.
