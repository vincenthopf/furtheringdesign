# Scope: Design Reasoning Framework

## Topic
How expert designers reason through design decisions — the cognitive operations, tradeoff resolution strategies, and judgment patterns that distinguish expert design from rule-following — and how to encode these as reasoning frameworks for LLM consumption without collapsing them into brittle rules.

## Parameters
- Budget: standard (~$0.50-$1.50 target)
- Depth: deep — this is the central intellectual challenge of the project
- Angles: 5 cycle-1 angles (see below)

## Prior Knowledge Bundles
- `.building/knowledge-bundles/ai-design-thinking/knowledge-bundle.md` — foundational research on design cognition, expert-novice differences, AI architecture, training data. Establishes the territory. This bundle drills into the specific problem of encoding reasoning patterns.

## Cycle 1 Angles (core tier, 5 queries)
1. Expert reasoning patterns beyond rules (abductive, co-evolution, framing, satisficing)
2. Design pedagogy — how schools teach judgment without rules
3. Tradeoff reasoning — how designers resolve competing concerns
4. LLM performance under reasoning frameworks vs rules in creative tasks
5. Lightweight rationale systems that actually worked in practice

## Likely Cycle 2 Gaps
- Encoding reasoning as prompts vs rules (the actual mechanism)
- Nondeterministic judgment and designer disagreement zones
- Intent-anchored reasoning that preserves creative exploration
- The hard constraint / soft heuristic / open reasoning spectrum
