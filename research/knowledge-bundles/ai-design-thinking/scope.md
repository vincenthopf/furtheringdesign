# Scope: Teaching AI the Design Thinking Process

## Topic (as stated by user)
How can we teach AI the thought process behind producing good design?
AI can copy design patterns but design *thinking* — the reasoning, the judgment, the intent — is never built in.
The current UI skill demonstrates this: it has rules but no reasoning. It produces output that looks like design but isn't *thought through* like design.

## Context
- The furtheringdesign project is building tools/skills that help AI produce genuinely good UI
- The existing UI skill (SKILL.md + design-guidelines/) encodes pattern rules (Tailwind, spacing, color) but not design reasoning
- The gap: a designer doesn't just apply patterns — they ask why, they hold hierarchy in their head, they resolve tension between competing priorities, they sense when something is "off"
- Goal: understand what design thinking actually IS, how expert designers do it, and what data/frameworks/approaches could encode that reasoning into AI

## Research Angles (Cycle 1 — Breadth)
1. What is design thinking as a cognitive process? (not the IDEO process framework — the actual mental acts)
2. How do expert designers make decisions differently from novices? (expertise research, protocol studies)
3. What attempts exist to encode design judgment in AI? (academic + industry)
4. What training data or signal sources could teach design reasoning? (not just visual outputs — process data)
5. What can we learn from design critique, annotation, and commentary as structured signal?
6. Are there cognitive science / situated cognition frameworks that explain design expertise?
